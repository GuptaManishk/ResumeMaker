Resume Types

Choosing a Resume Type

Resume Maker Guided Resumes offers you different types of resumes. Each type of resume is appropriate for different types of job-hunting situations as explained below.

Chronological

Chronological — This is the most common and straightforward resume. You list information in reverse chronological order with the most recent experience first. This kind of resume offers a clear, concise picture of you, and is the easiest to assimilate in a quick reading. A chronological resume contains objective, summary, experience, and education sections.

Who Should Use This Kind of Resume?

A chronological resume is useful when:

  • Your entire employment history shows progress with skills related to your objective.

  • You have a steady work record that reflects constant growth and movement.

  • Your most recent employment positions are impressive and work to your advantage.
Functional

Functional — This is a less common but effective resume. It emphasizes your qualifications and abilities and provides analyses of particular professional strengths. The employment strengths or skills are the important facts in a functional resume. A functional resume contains objective, accomplishments, capabilities, employment history, and education sections.

Who Should Use This Kind of Resume?

A functional resume is designed to be selective. It is useful when:

  • Your entire work history includes additional skills not related to your career objective.

  • You want to highlight only those skills pertinent to your objective.

  • You are a new college graduate, ex-military, seasoned professional, or changing careers.

  • You have work history gaps or special-issue problems.

 

Combination

Combination — A combination resume is a combination of the chronological and functional approaches. You can list information in a chronological format and also highlight your accomplishments. A combination resume contains objective, summary, accomplishments, experience, and education sections.

Who Should Use This Kind of Resume?

A combination resume brings attention to the jobs most directly related to your career objectives. It is useful when:

  • You are a new college graduate, ex-military, seasoned professional or changing careers.

  • Your work history has peaks of great success.

  • You want to highlight your experiences as well as your accomplishments and skills.

 

Targeted

In addition to the main resume types listed above, a resume can be targeted. A targeted resume can be any of the resume types above (it usually falls into the category of combination) that has been tailored to a specific occupation. Depending on which resume you select, you will see different combinations of sections which are recommended for each specific occupation.

Who Should Use This Kind of Resume?

A targeted resume is tailor-made for specific professions. It is useful for people in distinctive fields, such as teachers, computer professionals, attorneys, etc.
