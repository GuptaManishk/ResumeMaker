The Main Menu

From the Main Menu, you can access any part of the program quickly and can easily control all aspects of your job search. The Main Menu looks like this.

The Main Menu divides Resume Maker into eight main areas: Resumes, Cover Letters, Contact Manager, Virtual Interview, Salary Finder, Expert Advice, Resume Caster, and Job Finder. To take a closer look at any one of these areas, click a button on the left side of the Main Menu or click one of the icons at the top of the window.

Resumes

Guided Resumes takes you through the process of creating your resume. The Guide will prompt you with cards to enter the information for your resume. It is that simple. Answer each of the requests for information or pick from hundreds of thousands of pre-written resume phrases. Your resume will be formatted and completed for you. For more information see Creating a Resume.

Cover Letters

Guided Letters allows you to choose how your letters should look. You simply select from pre-written paragraphs and the Guide puts them together in a professional looking letter. For more information see Creating a Guided Letter.

Contact Manager

Contact Manager helps you keep track of all your job prospects. You can use it to keep track of target companies, contacts to whom you are sending resumes and cover letters, appointments you have scheduled, and To Do items you have to complete. You also have a calendar to keep you organized. For more information see Contact Manager.

Virtual Interview

View sample interviews and find out how to respond to over 500 job interview questions. You’ll learn all of this and more through videos, narration, and helpful on-screen interactions. For more information see Virtual Interview Overview.

Salary Finder

Find salary ranges based on job title and geographic location. Salary Finder can show you how much your peers are making or what a career change could mean to your paycheck. For more information see Find Your Salary Range

Expert Advice

Special features include hot tips and useful information about interviewing, questions you will need to be prepared to answer, and advice on interviewing etiquette. Find out what works and what doesn't work when searching for a job. For more information see Displaying Expert Advice.

Resume Caster

Submit your resume to every major career Web site on the World Wide Web, for review by thousands of hiring employers. Select the career Web sites of your choice and choose a level of security that keeps your personal information confidential. At any time you may edit, update, or delete your resume. Job Search Agents are available in Resume Caster to allow you to submit your name and e-mail address to a career Web site and be automatically notified when jobs that meet your specification are posted to the site. For more information see Resume Caster Overview.

Job Finder

Job Finder helps you search for a job through every major job listing on the Internet. Resume Maker can easily connect your computer to the World Wide Web and allow you to search millions of job openings. You can quickly search online by keywords or phrases for specific types of jobs. For more information see Job Finder Overview.

In addition, there are three buttons in the lower right and left sections of the Main Menu: User, Help, and Exit.

User

User changes the name of the individual user. You can have more than one user working on their own resume. You can also register your software if you didn't complete the online registration.

Help

Help opens the Resume Maker online Help system. You can also press (F1) anywhere in the program to get context-sensitive help on any command or feature.

Exit

Exit enables you to exit Resume Maker. You can also exit by pressing (Alt+F4).
